Farm animals

Harley Farms is one of the very few farmstead dairies in the United States. We raise milking goats and make cheese from their milk on the farm. This way, we manage every aspect of the outstanding taste of our cheeses. - type: 2-col img: "/img/about/animals_goats.jpg" hr: true orientation: left text: | ### Alpine goats Dee's first six goats were American Alpines, and today's herd are their descendants. American Alpines are a hardy, exceptionally agile breed with a gentle and curious temperament. They are an excellent milking breed, with a high yield of milk rich in butterfat and protein, which is ideal for making cheese. We milk the mature goats once a day, first thing. Each goat will produce a gallon of milk a day. That gallon makes one pound of cheese. We begin breeding and stop milking in fall, and babies arrive from mid-February to late May. - type: text hr: true markdown: false text: |

Other animals

Llamas

The llamas are guard animals. They will protect their territory and herd of goats from coyotes, dogs, and other intruders, by chasing the intruder and/or herding the flock into a safer place.

Chikens

We have a large flock of Leghorns and a handful of other breeds, and sell farm eggs in the cheese shop.

Barn Cats

Where there is grain, there are rats. There are several cats on the farm. If you see them asleep, encourage them to get up and get back to work.

Rosie the donkey

We adopted Rosie in 2014. She enjoys the company of the younger goats, though not necessarily the bucks. Rosie will look at you with a pleading, hungry face, but please resist feeding her your picnic. She is much too fat and needs a strict diet and plenty of walks. When the path is not sodden, we take her for evening walks through the farm Secret Garden. She is training to lead a donkey cart, and starred in the 2016 Palm Sunday parade in Redwood City.

Bees

The beehives are in our orchard on the path to the Secret Garden, so that the bees have miles of protected foraging space. Our farming neighbors have a field of organic mint alongside our garden. Farm honey makes a sweet wedding favor.

Dogs

The farm is the perfect home for old dogs, and we have adopted many over the years, most recently from Muttville senior dog rescue in San Francisco.
